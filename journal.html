📜 The InstAlert Rulebook — Non-Negotiable
01
No Trigger = No Trade. If none of the 6 institutional stress triggers are active, do absolutely nothing. The market is just noise. Protecting capital is a valid and profitable action.
02
Single Trigger = Half Size. Maximum 1% of capital (₹1,000 on ₹1L). One trigger firing without confirmation is hypothesis, not edge.
03
Double Trigger = Full Size. Maximum 2% of capital (₹2,000 on ₹1L) per trade when 2+ triggers fire simultaneously. This is the highest-conviction setup.
04
Stop-Loss at 30% Premium Loss. If you paid ₹120 for an option, your stop is ₹84. No exceptions. No "let me hold a bit more." Exit automatically.
05
Never Buy Options in Final 7 Days. Theta decay accelerates violently. You are fighting time and the market maker simultaneously. This rule alone saves 20% of losses.
06
5-Day Time Stop. If the trade thesis hasn't played out within 5 trading days, exit regardless of P&L. Institutional stress events are sharp and brief, not multi-week.
07
Book 50% at 1R, Let Rest Run. When premium doubles (2x entry), sell half position. Move stop-loss to breakeven on remaining. Protect the win while letting it grow.
08
IV Rank > 70 = Sell, Not Buy. When premiums are expensive (T5 setup), you are on the wrong side of the trade buying options. Sell premium instead — Iron Condor or Strangle.
09
Never Add to a Losing Trade. "Averaging down" on an options position is how retail accounts get wiped. If the trigger was right and market moved against you — the trigger was premature. Exit and wait for re-entry.
10
Review Every Trade Within 24 Hours. Log rationale, what you expected vs what happened, which rule was followed or broken. The journal is the product. The trades are just data.
