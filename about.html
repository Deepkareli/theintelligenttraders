How The Triggers Were Born

Each trigger came from a specific real-world observation

Not from backtesting software. Not from YouTube. From 12 years of lived institutional intelligence.

T1
VaR Breach — VIX Spike
Came from understanding how bank treasury desks manage daily loss limits. When VIX spikes beyond a threshold, the risk system fires automatically — there is no discretion. The desk must reduce positions regardless of their view. This forced mechanical selling is observable in VIX data in real time. Validated: COVID March 2020 (VIX 25→64), Russia-Ukraine February 2022 (+50% single week).
Source: Bank treasury VaR framework · NSE VIX daily data
T2
FII Drawdown Limit
Came from understanding how global fund mandates work. When FII net short futures cross ₹15,000 crore for multiple consecutive days, they are in drawdown management mode — mechanically reducing exposure per internal rules regardless of market view. Validated: October 2024 — record ₹1.14 lakh crore FII selling, driven entirely by mandate rules not India fundamentals.
Source: Global fund mandate structures · NSE FII participant data (published daily)
T3
Max Pain Expiry Pin
Came from understanding how options market makers delta-hedge their books. Their hedging activity on expiry day creates a mechanical gravitational pull toward the strike where maximum open interest sits — not manipulation, rule-following. Accuracy: 60–70% in calm markets. Fires every single expiry — the most calendar-predictable trigger in the system.
Source: Options market-making delta hedging mechanics · NSE option chain OI data
T4
Margin Call Cascade
Came from understanding how leveraged positions unwind. When all sectors fall simultaneously with no news catalyst and advance-decline ratio drops below 0.20 — it is not fundamental selling. It is forced liquidation to meet margin calls. The signature: unrelated sectors (IT, pharma, FMCG, banks) all falling equally. Validated: June 4, 2024 — Nifty -5.93%, fully recovered in 27 days.
Source: Margin call mechanics · NSE advance-decline data · Sector correlation analysis
T5
Pre-Event IV Crush
Came from understanding how institutional hedging desks buy options as portfolio insurance before known events and sell immediately after — regardless of outcome. This mechanical post-event de-hedging collapses implied volatility 30–50% within 30 minutes of every RBI policy, Budget, and major election result. Fires 8–10 times per year. The single most reliable trigger in the system — rated 10/10.
Source: Institutional portfolio hedging mechanics · IV Rank data · Every RBI policy event 2019–2024
T6
Macro FII Rule Trigger
Came from understanding how global EM allocation models work. When USDINR crosses key levels or US 10-year yields breach thresholds, global funds automatically reduce India weight per mandate — nothing to do with India fundamentals. Retail sees "India selling" and panics; the reality is a rule in a spreadsheet in New York triggered a non-discretionary reallocation. Validated: October 2024, October 2022, March 2020.
Source: Global EM allocation model mechanics · USDINR levels · US 10Y yield thresholds
